Website Look and Feel

The message that our website is giving out is to help you find clubs that you are interested in.

Our theme for our website is to make it look similar to the school website. We did this by following the same colors that they used. We made the background light blue, the text white, and the text/image boxes greyish blue

https://blog.kissmetrics.com/psychology-of-color-and-conversions/

Research has shown that a lot of people like the color blue, and blue also stands for trust and calmness. For example, facebook also uses the color blue because they want their audience to trust them. Since our website also uses blue as the main color, it can help build trust in the audience so that they will want to join a club.

Also, by doing this, our website will have a calm look because we used cool colors to make the website
look clean but still have color. If we used sharp colors such as bright red it would distract the
audience.

Template Wireframe

Template Wireframe

Parts in red will not change.

Parts in green will change on each subpage.

I wanted the template to have parts that can be edited and parts that can not so it will be easier when making the sub pages. By having elements in the template that can not be changed, the user will still be familiar with the site even when they change pages.

Wireframe Research

https://blog.kissmetrics.com/eye-tracking/
Research has shown that our attention on a website is to look at the middle first, and then the left corner. Also, we should put the more important things ABOVE the fold of the website. Lastly, our brains are wired to pay attention to human faces. With this information in mind, I put the video in the middle of the page. When they look at the middle of the page and see the video, they will see a face, which will focus them more onto the page. I also put the video and description of the club above the fold, because they are the most important. IF they are still interest, they will scroll down and see the contact information.
